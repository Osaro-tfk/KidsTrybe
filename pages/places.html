Popular Places

Palais Garnier

Palais Garnier is one of the most opulent Beaux-Arts buildings in Paris. Prepare to be dazzled by the grand marble staircase, gilded chandeliers, and frescoed ceilings.

Pont Alexandre III

This ornate, Beaux-Arts–style bridge connects the Left and Right Banks of Paris, and is one of the biggest tourist photo-ops in the city. We get it: With all those gilded statues and river views, the selfie urge is real.

Place De La Concorde

Despite its macabre history (it was the site of guillotine executions during the French Revolution), this public square is now one of the most beautiful places in Paris. Landmarks like the Luxor Obelisk and mermaid-filled fountain only add to the glamour.

Pantheon

The Panthéon houses the remains of scores of famous French figures, from Victor Hugo to Marie Curie. History aside, the breathtaking architecture draws a crowd.

The Man in the Wall

“The passer through the wall” is a statue that is seemingly half in and half out of the wall’ Although, popular in French literature most people may not have heard the story of “Le Passe-Muraille” by Marcel Aymé which is all about a man named M. Dutilleul who discovers that he can walk through walls.

Point Zéro

Point Zero is the traditional epicentre of Paris and this point marks the point of distance to anywhere else around the country. Nowadays, it has become a sort of legendary spot to ‘wish’ or request certain things.

Curious Dodo Manège

The Dodo Manège is a beautiful and unique carousel comprising of extinct and endangered animals. This attraction is relatively new to Paris. Only erected in 1992, Dodo Manège is situated relatively close to the Natural History Museum.

Flame of Liberty

Fancy seeing an iconic piece of New York City while in Paris? As you may know, France offered the Statue of Liberty as a gift to the United States, with the Flame of Liberty showcasing this friendship on French soil.
